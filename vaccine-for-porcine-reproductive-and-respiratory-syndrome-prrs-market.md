<p><p>The Vaccine for Porcine Reproductive and Respiratory Syndrome (PRRS) market features two main types: live vaccines and inactivated vaccines. Live vaccines use attenuated strains of the virus, stimulating a strong immune response with long-lasting protection but carrying a slight risk of reversion to virulence. In contrast, inactivated vaccines contain killed virus particles, ensuring safety and stability, though they may require multiple doses for effective immunity. Together, these vaccine types aim to mitigate PRRS's impact on swine health and production.</p></p>

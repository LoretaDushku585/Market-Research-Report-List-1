<p><p>Manual welders play a significant role in various welding techniques, primarily projection and spot welding. Projection welding involves joining overlapping metal parts by applying heat and pressure through a localized projection, creating strong bonds suitable for thicker materials. In contrast, spot welding uses electrodes to deliver concentrated heat to specific points, ideal for joining thin sheets of metal quickly. Both methods are essential in industries like automotive and manufacturing, offering efficiency and versatility in creating durable connections.</p></p>

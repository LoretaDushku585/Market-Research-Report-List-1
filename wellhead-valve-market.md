<p><p>The wellhead valve market is divided into two primary types: manual and automatic valves. Manual valves require operators to control the flow of fluids manually, offering simplicity and direct control, making them suitable for smaller or less complex systems. In contrast, automatic valves use sensors and actuators for remote operation, enhancing efficiency and safety, particularly in large-scale operations where real-time adjustments are necessary. The choice between these valve types often depends on the specific operational requirements and technological advancements in the field.</p></p>

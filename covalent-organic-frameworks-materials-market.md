<p><p>Covalent Organic Frameworks (COFs) are classified into two primary market types: two-dimensional (2D) and three-dimensional (3D) materials. </p><p>2D COFs consist of flat, layered structures that offer high surface area and tunable properties, making them ideal for applications like catalysis and gas storage. </p><p>In contrast, 3D COFs feature interconnected networks that enhance stability and pore accessibility, suitable for applications in drug delivery and separation processes. Both types exhibit unique characteristics that cater to diverse industrial needs and research applications.</p></p>

<p><p>The food grade glucose market primarily includes two types: monohydrate glucose and anhydrous glucose. Monohydrate glucose contains one molecule of water, resulting in higher moisture content, making it suitable for products requiring hydration, such as baked goods and syrups. Anhydrous glucose, on the other hand, is free of water, providing a more concentrated form. It is often used in dry formulations, candies, and pharmaceuticals due to its stability and longer shelf life, catering to diverse industry needs.</p></p>

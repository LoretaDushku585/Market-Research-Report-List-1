<p><p>The organic phosphinate flame retardant market is primarily segmented into phosphate and phosphite types. Phosphates are esters of phosphoric acid, known for their effectiveness in preventing ignition and slowing down combustion. They are widely used in polymers and textiles. Phosphites, on the other hand, are derived from phosphorous acid and often act as secondary flame retardants, enhancing thermal stability and reducing smoke emissions. Both types play crucial roles in improving fire safety across various applications, including construction materials and electronics.</p></p>
